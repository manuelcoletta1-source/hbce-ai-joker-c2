## 1. Purpose

This document defines the dual-use risk register of AI JOKER-C2.

AI JOKER-C2 is the identity-bound cognitive command runtime of the HERMETICUM B.C.E. ecosystem.

It connects three primary domains:

1. MATRIX
2. CORPUS ESOTEROLOGIA ERMETICA
3. APOKALYPSIS

The purpose of this register is to classify dual-use risk, distinguish authorized civil and strategic uses from prohibited uses, and define when the runtime should allow, audit, escalate, degrade or block a request.

Dual-use is used here in a controlled civil and strategic sense.

It does not mean unrestricted use.

It does not authorize offensive capability.

It does not authorize unlawful surveillance, exploitation, sabotage, coercion, manipulation or autonomous targeting.

Within AI JOKER-C2, dual-use means that the same governance runtime may support both civil and strategic environments when the use remains lawful, defensive, accountable and traceable.

Authorized dual-use includes:

defensive cybersecurity;

critical infrastructure resilience documentation;

cloud and data governance;

compliance-oriented documentation;

operational continuity;

audit and evidence generation;

institutional reporting;

B2B and B2G governance workflows.


Prohibited dual-use includes:

offensive cyber operations;

malware;

exploit deployment;

credential theft;

unauthorized access;

unlawful surveillance;

sabotage;

autonomous targeting;

disinformation operations;

coercive manipulation;

repression of rights;

extremist operational support.


Dual-use must remain governed.

4. Dual-Use Risk Classes

Use the following dual-use risk classes.

Risk Class	Meaning	Default Handling

DU-LOW	Civil, documentation or low-impact governance use	ALLOW
DU-MEDIUM	Strategic or institutional relevance requiring auditability	AUDIT
DU-HIGH	Sensitive dual-use context requiring human review	ESCALATE or DEGRADE
DU-CRITICAL	High-impact or regulated context requiring strict review	ESCALATE or BLOCK
DU-PROHIBITED	Offensive, abusive, unlawful or outside scope	BLOCK
DU-UNKNOWN	Cannot be safely classified	ESCALATE or BLOCK


Unknown dual-use risk must not become permission.


---

5. Dual-Use Decision Values

Decision	Meaning

ALLOW	Operation may proceed
AUDIT	Operation may proceed but should be recorded or reviewed
ESCALATE	Human review, legal review, security review or institutional authority required
DEGRADE	Only limited safe support is allowed
BLOCK	Operation is prohibited or unsafe
NOOP	No operational action taken


The decision must follow risk, policy, authority and human oversight.

18. Dual-Use Review Workflow

Identify use case
-> classify project domain
-> classify context
-> classify intent
-> assign dual-use risk
-> define authorized scope
-> detect prohibited scope
-> assign decision
-> assign human oversight
-> define mitigation
-> determine EVT requirement
-> assign owner
-> audit or escalate
-> close, mitigate or block

Sensitive dual-use operations should not proceed without review.

19. Fail-Closed Rule

Fail-closed must activate when:

dual-use risk is unknown in a sensitive context;

prohibited scope is detected;

human oversight is required but absent;

policy cannot be applied;

project domain cannot be classified;

authority is unclear;

data sensitivity is unknown;

output may enable abuse;

output may affect real systems;

output may imply certification or adoption without basis;

EVT cannot be generated for a significant operation.


Fail-closed rule:

Uncertainty in dual-use operations does not authorize execution.
