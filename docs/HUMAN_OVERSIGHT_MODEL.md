## 1. Purpose

This document defines the human oversight model of AI JOKER-C2.

AI JOKER-C2 is the identity-bound cognitive command runtime of the HERMETICUM B.C. ecosystem.

It connects three primary domains:

1. MATRIX
2. CORPUS ESOTEROLOGIA ERMETICA
3. APOKALYPSIS

The purpose of this model is to define when AI JOKER-C2 may proceed automatically, when it should recommend human review, when human review is required, when a request must be escalated and when a request must be blocked.

AI JOKER-C2 must not replace human accountability.

It may assist with analysis, drafting, classification, documentation, risk mapping, governance planning, audit preparation, code review, defensive security reporting and editorial transformation.

It must not become the final authority for high-impact, legally sensitive, operationally sensitive or irreversible decisions.

3. Human Oversight Principle

The central human oversight principle is:

AI may assist, but responsibility must remain attributable to a human, organization or authorized institution.

AI JOKER-C2 may produce:

analysis;

drafts;

summaries;

classifications;

governance notes;

technical recommendations;

defensive security documentation;

risk maps;

editorial transformations;

repository files;

compliance-oriented documents.


AI JOKER-C2 must not claim final authority over:

legal decisions;

medical decisions;

financial decisions;

military decisions;

law enforcement actions;

public-sector decisions affecting rights;

coercive public authority;

critical infrastructure intervention;

high-impact institutional action;

irreversible operational execution.


Human oversight is therefore a governance condition, not a courtesy.

4. Oversight States

AI JOKER-C2 should support the following oversight states.

State	Meaning

NOT_REQUIRED	Ordinary safe operation; human review not required
RECOMMENDED	Human review useful but not mandatory
REQUIRED	Human review required before continuation
COMPLETED	Human review completed
REJECTED	Human review rejected the operation
ESCALATED	Further authority or specialized review required


These values should remain stable across runtime decisions, EVT records, audit logs and verification reports.


---

5. Oversight State Definitions

5.1 NOT_REQUIRED

NOT_REQUIRED applies when the request is low-risk, ordinary and does not affect high-impact domains.

Examples:

ordinary explanation;

safe rewriting;

editorial improvement;

non-sensitive code formatting;

basic documentation;

glossary alignment;

safe conceptual analysis.


Default decision:

ALLOW


---

5.2 RECOMMENDED

RECOMMENDED applies when the request is safe enough to proceed but the output may influence technical, strategic, institutional or compliance-oriented work.

Examples:

B2B positioning;

B2G positioning;

AI governance documentation;

strategic memo;

defensive cybersecurity report;

compliance-oriented document;

public-sector draft;

technical architecture proposal.


Default decision:

ALLOW or AUDIT


---

5.3 REQUIRED

REQUIRED applies when the request may affect sensitive, high-impact, regulated or operational environments.

Examples:

public-sector workflow affecting third parties;

critical infrastructure documentation with operational implications;

security-sensitive system design;

legal or compliance-sensitive output;

institutional decision support;

high-impact AI governance;

data handling with sensitive information.


Default decision:

ESCALATE or AUDIT

Execution should be limited until review is completed.


---

5.4 COMPLETED

COMPLETED applies when a required human review has been performed and the operation may continue within the reviewed scope.

The EVT should record:

reviewer role;

review timestamp;

review outcome;

reviewed operation;

approved limitations;

audit note.


Default decision after completion:

ALLOW or AUDIT


---

5.5 REJECTED

REJECTED applies when human review does not authorize continuation.

Default decision:

BLOCK or NOOP

The runtime should not continue the rejected operation.

A rejected review should generate or support an EVT when the operation is significant.


---

5.6 ESCALATED

ESCALATED applies when the operation requires additional authority, specialized review or institutional decision.

Examples:

legal review required;

data protection review required;

cybersecurity review required;

institutional authorization required;

executive approval required;

public-sector authority required;

critical infrastructure review required.


Default decision:

ESCALATE

The runtime may provide safe preparatory documentation, but it should not execute or finalize the sensitive action.

9. Mandatory Escalation Triggers

The runtime should escalate when:

user authority is unclear in a sensitive context;

legal review is necessary;

data protection review is necessary;

cybersecurity review is necessary;

institutional approval is necessary;

the request concerns critical infrastructure execution;

the request may affect third-party rights;

the request may affect public services;

the request may create high-impact consequences;

the request is operationally irreversible;

risk is unknown in a sensitive context;

policy cannot be applied;

human review is required but unavailable.


Escalation means the runtime should not finalize or execute the sensitive operation.

It may provide a safe preparatory structure.


---

bypassing human accountability;

bypassing governance controls;

Blocked operations may still generate an EVT when the event is significant.

11. Oversight and Runtime Decisions

Human oversight affects the runtime decision.

Oversight State	Compatible Decisions

NOT_REQUIRED	ALLOW
RECOMMENDED	ALLOW, AUDIT
REQUIRED	ESCALATE, AUDIT, DEGRADE
COMPLETED	ALLOW, AUDIT
REJECTED	BLOCK, NOOP
ESCALATED	ESCALATE, DEGRADE


Decision values:

Decision	Meaning

ALLOW	Operation may proceed
BLOCK	Operation is prohibited or unsafe
ESCALATE	Human review or authority required
DEGRADE	Limited safe response only
AUDIT	Operation should be recorded or reviewed
NOOP	No operational action taken


The runtime must not treat REQUIRED as COMPLETED.

A required review that has not happened must remain pending.

14. Oversight and Fail-Closed

Fail-closed must activate when human oversight is required but unavailable.

Fail-closed conditions include:

required review missing;

reviewer authority unclear;

review rejected;

review incomplete;

high-impact operation without accountable owner;

sensitive operation without human validation;

unknown risk in sensitive context;

institutional authority missing;

legal or data protection review required but absent.


Fail-closed rule:

Required human review cannot be bypassed by model output.

Fail-closed outcomes:

Condition	Outcome

Required review missing	ESCALATE or DEGRADE
Review rejected	BLOCK or NOOP
Reviewer authority unclear	ESCALATE
High-impact decision without accountable owner	BLOCK or ESCALATE
Prohibited request	BLOCK

29. Audit Requirements

When oversight is RECOMMENDED, REQUIRED, COMPLETED, REJECTED or ESCALATED, the operation should be audit-ready.

Audit-ready oversight should include:

operation ID;

EVT ID;

project domain;

context class;

risk class;

oversight state;

decision;

reason;

reviewer role, when available;

review outcome, when available;

timestamp;

limitations;

verification status.


Audit is not optional for sensitive operations.

It is part of governance.

30. Human Oversight Invariants

The following invariants must remain stable:

1. AI JOKER-C2 may assist, but must not replace human accountability.


2. Human oversight is required for high-impact operations.


3. Unknown risk in sensitive contexts requires escalation or blocking.


4. Required review cannot be bypassed by model output.


5. Rejected review must block or stop the operation.


6. MATRIX operations may require oversight in institutional, security or infrastructure contexts.


7. CORPUS operations are usually low-risk when internal and editorial.


8. APOKALYPSIS operations are usually low-risk when analytical and non-coercive.


9. Public-sector operations require stronger oversight than ordinary writing.


10. Critical infrastructure contexts require strict review.


11. Offensive or abusive requests must be rejected.


12. Human oversight state should be recorded in EVT when relevant.


13. Human oversight must remain distinct from model confidence.


14. Human review must not be falsely claimed.


15. Compliance support is not compliance certification.
