12. MATRIX and Fail-Closed Governance

MATRIX requires fail-closed behavior.

Fail-closed means that the runtime must block, degrade, audit or escalate when governance conditions are missing.

Fail-closed is triggered by:

missing identity;

unclear authority;

unknown risk;

missing policy;

missing project-domain classification;

missing human review in sensitive contexts;

unsafe technical request;

offensive cyber interpretation;

unlawful surveillance risk;

critical infrastructure sensitivity;

inability to generate EVT;

inability to verify continuity.


Rule:

Uncertainty in sensitive operations does not authorize execution.

Fail-closed is not a defect.

It is the safety boundary of MATRIX.

14. MATRIX Risk Categories

MATRIX risks usually involve infrastructure, institutions, cybersecurity, B2B, B2G, cloud, data, energy or critical systems.

Risk Category	Description

AI governance risk	AI output used without policy, risk or oversight
Institutional risk	Public-sector document used without human review
Cybersecurity risk	Defensive analysis drifting into offensive detail
Critical infrastructure risk	Documentation used as operational instruction without authority
Data governance risk	Sensitive data exposed or stored unnecessarily
Cloud governance risk	Responsibility boundary unclear
B2B risk	Enterprise capability overstated
B2G risk	Institutional adoption implied without proof
Compliance risk	Compliance orientation mistaken for certification
Strategic risk	Public positioning exceeds demonstrated capability


Default MATRIX handling:

Risk Level	Handling

LOW	ALLOW
MEDIUM	ALLOW or AUDIT
HIGH	ESCALATE or DEGRADE
CRITICAL	ESCALATE or BLOCK
PROHIBITED	BLOCK
