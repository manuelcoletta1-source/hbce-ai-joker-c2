6. Canonical Sequence

The canonical sequence is:

Decisione · Costo · Traccia · Tempo

Decisione

Decisione is the activation point of an operational sequence.

It marks the moment in which a configuration exits pure possibility and enters a determinate path.

Costo

Costo is the exposure produced by decision.

Every decision generates cost because every operational configuration consumes, excludes, displaces or transforms something.

Traccia

Traccia is the persistence of the event.

A decision that produces cost leaves a trace that can be reconstructed, contested, verified or inherited.

Tempo

Tempo is the verification field.

Only time shows whether a configuration persists, decays, transforms or collapses.

Together, these terms define the minimum grammar of operational reality.

11. Corpus Risk Categories

Corpus work is usually low-risk when it remains internal, theoretical and editorial.

Risk increases when Corpus output is used externally or framed as authority.

Risk Category	Description

Terminology drift	Canonical terms are changed inconsistently
Formula drift	DCTT is weakened, omitted or contradicted
Editorial fragmentation	Chapters lose continuity and structural flow
External claim risk	Internal theory is presented as external factual proof
Legal authority risk	Corpus language is presented as legal certification
Scientific authority risk	Internal theory is presented as validated science without review
Institutional authority risk	Corpus framing implies public or official endorsement
Coercive misuse risk	Conceptual framing is used to justify harm or control


Default handling:

Risk Level	Handling

LOW	ALLOW
MEDIUM	AUDIT
HIGH	ESCALATE
CRITICAL	ESCALATE or BLOCK
PROHIBITED	BLOCK
